02
The selection

What we're
looking for

There's no ideal profile. There's work that has something to say — and an artist who knows (or is figuring out) why they make it. Here are the concrete criteria behind every application review.

What matters

A recognisable voice

It doesn't need to be "never seen before." It needs to be yours. Work we could attribute to you even without a signature.

What matters

Internal coherence

Your works should speak to each other. No need for a perfect series — just a thread. A direction, even if still forming.

What matters

Ability to articulate

Can you explain what you do, even imperfectly? No critical essay needed — just two honest lines about why that work exists.

What doesn't

Followers & CV

We don't look at how many people follow you or how many shows you've done. We look at the work. Only the work.

Medium is secondary — painting, photography, sculpture, digital, textile, installation. We welcome artists from everywhere. You don't need to be Italian.

03
The process

From today
to live

No bureaucracy, no waiting in the dark. The process is simple and transparent — you always know where things stand.

1

You apply

Send an email with: who you are, a link to your work, 3–5 pieces that represent you, and a couple of lines about what you're looking for. Not a CV, not a formal cover letter. Be yourself — that's what we want to see.

Under 10 minutes
2

We review your work

We look at every application carefully. We reply within 2 weeks, always — even if the answer is no. If it's no, we tell you why, concretely. No automated messages.

Reply within 14 days
3

We get to know each other

If selected, we connect — DM or a short call — to understand your work better and what you want to communicate. We don't put you in a database: we build a story together. You're the source, we're the translators.

A 15–20 minute call
4

You go live

We publish your profile and your first post. We send you the link — share it however and wherever you want. You're the protagonist, we're the stage. From here, we build together.

Published in agreement with you
04
Your side

What we
ask of you

Free doesn't mean effortless. We ask a few things — all reasonable, none invasive. And nothing you wouldn't already have.

That the work is yours. Only original pieces you hold the rights to. No appropriation, no work by others presented as your own.

Readable images. At least 1500px on the long side. No professional photographer needed — good light and a clean frame are enough. Your work deserves to be seen properly.

A bit of you, not just the work. A few lines about your process, what you're exploring. Not a formal bio — your words, the way you'd say it to a friend.

A minimal online presence. Not thousands of followers — but an Instagram, a website, even just an active Behance. Something to point people to when they want to follow you.

And if you feel like it: share the profile when it goes live. Not mandatory — but it helps both of us. No other requirements. No exclusivity. Do whatever you want.
